About

Property Types

First, here's a look at the types of properties listed on Airbnb in San Francisco. As is clear on the graph to the left, Apartments are listed about twice as often as houses, which are in turn listed about twice as often as every other type put together.

To look at the graph of everything listed under "other", click here to swap between the graphs. Most of the listings here are fairly normal, but some like 'Lighthouse', 'Treehouse', 'Castle', etc. stand out - but when looking at the actual listings, it doesn't seem like any of them are literal.

Neighborhood Costs

Here's the average cost per night per neighborhood. Generally speaking, the northernmost neighborhood are the highest priced per night, but when you start moving away from there, the cost goes down.

For the whole city, the average price is per night. Comparing this with the average hotel room price for the city, Airbnb is far cheaper, and usually includes wifi and other various things that hotels tend to charge extra for.

Neighborhood ratings

Across the board, general ratings are really good.

A lot of analysis could be done on the ratings to find factors that suggest a higher rating. Various amenities, proximity to landmarks, etc. might tell some of the story, and sentiment analysis of the ratings could give the rest of it.
